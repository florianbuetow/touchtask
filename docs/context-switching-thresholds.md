# Context Switching & Interruption Thresholds

Based on research on context switching and interruption costs (e.g., Gloria Mark's work showing it takes ~23 minutes to refocus after an interruption).

## Key Distinction

- **Interruptions** are external — someone else broke your flow
- **Context switches** are self-inflicted — you chose to change task

Both are costly, but interruptions tend to be more disruptive because they're unexpected. That's why the thresholds for interruptions are lower.

These assume a typical 8-hour workday with ~4-5 hours of targeted deep work. If someone does fewer pomodoro sessions, the thresholds should be even lower.

## Interruptions

| Level    | Count | Color  | Rationale                                          |
|----------|-------|--------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Normal   | 0-3   | Green  | A few interruptions are unavoidable in any workday  |
| Warning  | 4-7   | Orange | You're losing 1.5-2.5 hours just to refocusing     |
| Critical | 8+    | Red    | More time recovering than doing deep work           |

## Context Switches

| Level    | Count | Color  | Rationale                                                                    |
|----------|-------|--------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Normal   | 0-4   | Green  | Switching between tasks at natural breakpoints (e.g., after a pomodoro)      |
| Warning  | 5-9   | Orange | Fragmented attention, struggling to finish anything                          |
| Critical | 10+   | Red    | Task-hopping — likely completing nothing meaningful                          |
